🇯🇵 21-Day Japanese Speaking Plan

Be polite, understood, and comfortable speaking in Japan
🗓️ WEEK 1 — Survival Confidence (Days 1–7)
Goal: Be polite, understood, and comfortable opening your mouth
Day 1 — Politeness Foundation
すみません
sumimasen
excuse me / sorry / thanks
ありがとうございます
arigatō gozaimasu
thank you
ありがとうございました
arigatō gozaimashita
thank you (after/past tense)
✅ These three phrases solve many situations alone. Practice saying each 3 times.
Day 2 — Choosing & Ordering
これをお願いします
kore wo onegaishimasu
this one, please
これと、これをお願いします
kore to, kore wo onegaishimasu
this and this, please
Practice pointing at imaginary items while speaking.
Day 3 — Limits & Comfort
大丈夫です
daijōbu desu
I'm okay / no thanks
今日はこれだけで
kyō wa kore dake de
just this today
Practice saying calmly, with a nod.
Day 4 — Asking for Help
すみません、ちょっといいですか？
sumimasen, chotto ii desu ka?
excuse me, may I ask?
助かりました
tasukarimashita
that helped a lot
Practice one slow, relaxed delivery.
Day 5 — Not Understanding (Very Important)
分かりません
wakarimasen
I don't understand
日本語は少しだけです
nihongo wa sukoshi dake desu
my Japanese is only a little
ゆっくりお願いします
yukkuri onegaishimasu
slowly, please
These reduce pressure instantly.
Day 6 — Eating Etiquette
いただきます
itadakimasu
before eating (let's eat)
ごちそうさまでした
gochisōsama deshita
after eating (thank you for the meal)
とても美味しかったです
totemo oishikatta desu
it was very delicious
Say these with warmth, not volume.
Day 7 — Mini Flow (Combine)
Say this mini sequence aloud:
すみません → これをお願いします → ありがとうございました → ごちそうさまでした
excuse me → this please → thank you → thanks for the meal
🎉 You can already eat confidently in Japan!
🗓️ WEEK 2 — Daily Life & Movement (Days 8–14)
Goal: Navigate shops, stations, and small talk
Day 8 — Directions
トイレはどこですか？
toire wa doko desu ka?
where is the toilet?
駅はどこですか？
eki wa doko desu ka?
where is the station?
Practice substituting words in the pattern: ~はどこですか？
Day 9 — Transportation
この電車は東京に行きますか？
kono densha wa tōkyō ni ikimasu ka?
does this train go to Tokyo?
分かりました
wakarimashita
I understand
Day 10 — Shops & Browsing
ちょっと見るだけです
chotto miru dake desu
just looking
あとで来ます
ato de kimasu
I'll come back later
Day 11 — Quantities & Additions
もう一つお願いします
mō hitotsu onegaishimasu
one more, please
少なめでお願いします
sukuname de onegaishimasu
smaller portion, please
Day 12 — Confirming & Correcting
はい
hai
yes
いいえ
iie
no
そうです
sō desu
that's right
違います
chigaimasu
that's not it
Day 13 — Social Ease
こんにちは
konnichiwa
hello / good afternoon
こんばんは
konbanwa
good evening
またね
mata ne
see you later (casual)
Day 14 — Realistic Role Play
Speak this out loud:
こんにちは → すみません → おすすめはどれですか → これをお願いします → ありがとうございました
hello → excuse me → what do you recommend? → this please → thank you
✅ This sounds completely natural in Japan!
🗓️ WEEK 3 — Confidence & Natural Flow (Days 15–21)
Goal: Speak without fear or hesitation
Day 15 — Recommendations
おすすめはどれですか？
osusume wa dore desu ka?
what do you recommend?
それでお願いします
sore de onegaishimasu
I'll go with that
Day 16 — Adjustments
レアでお願いします
rea de onegaishimasu
rare, please
軽く焼いてください
karuku yaite kudasai
lightly grilled, please
Day 17 — Time & Pacing
ちょっと待ってください
chotto matte kudasai
please wait a moment
大丈夫です、急いでいません
daijōbu desu, isoide imasen
I'm okay, no rush
Day 18 — Expressing Satisfaction
ちょうどいいです
chōdo ii desu
that's perfect
満足です
manzoku desu
I'm satisfied
Day 19 — Polite Exit (Very Important)
ごちそうさまでした。ありがとうございました。
gochisōsama deshita. arigatō gozaimashita.
thank you for the meal. thank you very much.
Practice saying it slowly, warmly, with a slight bow.
Day 20 — Full Interaction Rehearsal
Say this naturally, without stopping:
すみません → 日本語は少しだけです → おすすめはどれですか → これをお願いします → とても美味しかったです → ごちそうさまでした
excuse me → my Japanese is only a little → what do you recommend? → this please → it was very delicious → thank you for the meal
Day 21 — Confidence Day
Repeat only what feels useful. Speak calmly. Smile.
🎉 You are now functionally conversational in daily Japan life!
🌟 One phrase to remember forever
If all else fails:
すみません、日本語は少しだけです
sumimasen, nihongo wa sukoshi dake desu
excuse me, my Japanese is only a little
This makes people kinder, slower, and more helpful.
