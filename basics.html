Food Word Patterns
-yaki = grilled
Anything with "yaki" at the end means it's grilled or cooked on a griddle.
yaki-niku 焼肉 → grilled meat
tako-yaki たこ焼き → grilled octopus balls
okonomi-yaki お好み焼き → savory pancake
teppan-yaki 鉄板焼き → grill-plate cooking
-niku = meat
Refers to meat in general (beef or pork).
yaki-niku 焼肉 → grilled meat
niku-dōfu 肉豆腐 → beef with tofu
niku-man 肉まん → meat bun
-gyū = beef (specifically)
When you see "gyū", it specifically means beef.
wa-gyū 和牛 → Japanese beef
gyū-don 牛丼 → beef rice bowl
-don = bowl (rice bowl)
Pattern: [topping] + don = rice bowl with that topping.
gyū-don 牛丼 → beef bowl
ten-don 天丼 → tempura bowl
katsu-don カツ丼 → pork cutlet bowl
-age = fried
Deep-fried or fried items end with "age".
kara-age 唐揚げ → Japanese fried chicken
tori-age 鳥揚げ → fried chicken
age-dashi 揚げ出し → fried and served in broth
-men = noodles
All noodle dishes end with "men".
ra-men ラーメン → ramen
u-don うどん → thick wheat noodles
so-men そうめん → thin noodles
